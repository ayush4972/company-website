The problem

A broken road, and nowhere to report it

A pothole, a broken streetlight, a blocked drain — everyone sees them, but reporting one used to mean a phone call to the right office, on the right day, to the right person. Most reports never made it, and the ones that did vanished into a queue no citizen could see.

Sadak Sujhav closes that loop. Anyone with a phone can report a road issue in seconds, watch it move through the system, and see it resolved — while municipalities get a single, organized stream of what's broken and where.
